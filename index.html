Slide 2 · Journey, Pain Points & Product Vision

Where Reordering Breaks Today

Carlos runs two stores, lives on his phone, and has 20–30 seconds between customers. The product must think for him.

Carlos’ Reorder Journey

  1. Need detection — shelves run low or competitor has something teens love.
  2. Open RepRally — tiny window before the next customer arrives.
  3. Reorder — wants “my usual” plus “what’s hot with kids now”, minimal edits.
  4. Discovery (optional) — only if risk is clearly bounded.
  5. Checkout — needs clarity on price, margin, and delivery timing.

Product Vision

“Always-on core. Guided experiments. Zero thinking.”

  • Core SKUs never stock out.
  • Each order surfaces 2–3 obvious, data-backed adds.
  • Experiments are small, capped, and auto-reviewed.
  • Everything fits inside <30 seconds on mobile.

Top Pain Points

  • Prioritized(Severity, Frequency, Business Impact, nderserved):
    • P1 – No signal on when to reorder → repeated stockouts on winners.
    • P2 – Catalog overload (500+ SKUs) → defaults to “same as last time”.
    • P3 – No time for research → if it’s not 1–2 taps, it won’t happen.
    • P4 – Fear of dead stock → experiments feel risky; bad bets kill trust.
    • P5 – Two different customer bases → high-school vs neighborhood store treated the same.

    Business Model Alignment

    • RepRally is the matching & intelligence layer for brands and stores.
    • We win when reorders are frequent, high-value, and low-risk.
    • The Reorder Experience is where GMV, margin, and trust are created.
    Smart Reorder · MVP
    Slide 3 · MVP · 0–6 Weeks

    Core-first Smart Reorder

    Simple, deterministic v1 that prevents stockouts, speeds ordering, and removes cognitive load.

    What Carlos Sees

    • Don’t Run Out — red strip showing SKUs with <3–5 days of cover.
    • Smart Reorder List — pre-filled quantities based on last successful order.
    • High School Picks v0 — 2–3 snack ideas that sell well in nearby teen-heavy stores.

    Why This Works

    • P1 — Timing solved: days-of-cover = (inventory ÷ avg daily velocity). “Don’t run out” removes mental math.
    • P2 — Catalog overload: default entry is a short, ranked list — not 500 SKUs.
    • P3 — No time: designed for <30 sec — glance → accept → tweak → submit.
    • P4 — Fear of dead stock: HS Picks v0 limited to low-risk, high-sell-through SKUs.

    Business Metrics

    • North Star: Reorder GMV per active store.
    • Operational: reorder rate/store/month, % fast movers with no stockout, median time to reorder.
    • Revenue: AOV (reorders), gross margin/store, dead-stock rate (>90 days).
    Smarter Recommendations · V2
    Slide 4 · V2 · Smarter Recs & Autopilot

    From Good Reorders to Great Assortments

    V2 deepens forecasting, personalization, and two-store orchestration, and adds Autopilot for heroes.

    Better Stock-out Modeling

    • Rolling 90-day demand + scan frequency → more accurate days of cover.
    • Prioritize items by stockout risk × margin contribution.

    Store-level Personalization

    • Teen-heavy stores: prioritize sweet snacks & beverages.
    • Office-heavy stores: emphasize coffee, protein, functional HBW.

    Two-store Orchestration

    • Side-by-side store recommendations.
    • One-tap action: “Mirror 1289 order to 22401 (×2)” when patterns align.

    Experiment Outcome Card

    • “You tried 8 new items last quarter → 3 made money, 2 broke even, 3 tied up capital. Keep A, B; drop C, D, E.”

    Autopilot for Fast Movers

    • “Keep 21 days of stock for Realsy — auto reorder when needed.”
    • System monitors velocity and places draft orders automatically.
    • Carlos only confirms payment.
    • Targets P1 (timing) and P3 (time pressure) with very high impact.

    RepRally AI · Reorder Assistant
    Slide 5 · Future · RepRally AI

    “What Should I Restock Today?”

    Voice-first assistant that turns manual browsing into AI-guided, bundle-based reordering.

    Experience Flow

    • Listening — “Hey RepRally AI, what should I restock today?”
    • Analysis — checks stockout risk, velocity, trends, peer spikes, weather.
    • Summary — cards for “Don’t run out”, “Maximize profit”, “Trending near you”, “Weather demand alert”.

    What RepRally AI Optimizes

    • Low-stock prediction — “days left” per SKU, confidence threshold.
    • High-margin picks — margin × units sold, uplift vs current substitutes, promos.
    • Trend & micro-segment intelligence — neighborhood type, maps meta, peer demand.
    • Weather & seasonal effects — heatwaves, rain, sports events, etc.
    • Voice-to-order — “reorder my best-sellers”, “show high-margin SKUs”, “what’s trending near me?”.

    Edge Cases & Safety

    • No history → use store type + demographic defaults.
    • Multi-store owners → prompt for store selection.
    • Low-confidence suggestions → escalate to rep verification.
    • Owner disagrees → explicit feedback loop into ranking models.
    Slide 6 · Interview-style Summary

    How This System Serves Carlos — and RepRally

    I built the reorder experience around Carlos’ real behavior — fast movers he must protect, teen trends he wants but can’t research, and experiments he fears because of dead stock.

    The Flow

    • Smart Core Reorder removes cognitive load around timing and quantities.
    • High School Trends drives relevant, high-margin discovery.
    • Guardrails for experiments grow baskets without tying up capital.
    • Two-store mode keeps both locations in sync with one brain.
    • Autopilot & RepRally AI are the natural evolution for retention and leverage.

    Impact on the Flywheel

    • More high-quality reorders → more behavioral & demand data.
    • Better data → smarter recommendations and pricing.
    • Smarter recommendations → higher GMV, margin, and loyalty.
    • Higher GMV & retention → more brands, better assortment, stronger marketplace.
