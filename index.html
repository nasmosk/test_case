Slide 2 · Journey, Pain Points & Product Thesis

Where Reordering Breaks Today

Carlos has two stores, lives on his phone, and has 20–30 seconds between customers. The product has to think for him.

Carlos’ Reorder Journey

  1. Need detection – Sees shelves running low or a competitor carrying something teens love.
  2. Open app / link – Has a tiny window before the next customer.
  3. Reorder action – Wants “my usual” plus “what’s hot with kids now”, then quick edits.
  4. Discovery (optional) – Only if risk is clearly bounded.
  5. Checkout – Needs clarity on price, margin, and delivery timing.

Product Thesis

“Always-on core. Guided experiments. Zero thinking.”

  • Never stock out of hero SKUs (Realsy, Magic Mind, Behave).
  • Every reorder suggests 2–3 no-brainer adds based on similar stores.
  • Experiments are small, time-boxed, auto-reviewed.
  • The full flow fits inside <30 seconds on mobile.

Top Pain Points (Prioritized)

  • P1 — No signal on when to reorder → repeated stockouts on winners.
  • P2 — Catalog overload (500+ SKUs) → defaults to “same as last time”.
  • P3 — No time for research → if it’s not 1–2 taps, it won’t happen.
  • P4 — Fear of dead stock → experiments feel risky; bad bets kill trust.
  • P5 — Two different customer bases → high-school vs neighborhood store treated the same.

Business Model Alignment

  • RepRally is the matching & intelligence layer for brands and stores.
  • You win when reorders are frequent, high-value, and low-risk.
  • The Reorder Experience is where GMV, margin and trust are created.
Carlos · Smart Reorder (MVP)
Slide 3 · Smart Reorder MVP (0–6 weeks)

Core-first Smart Reorder · Ship Fast, Move GMV

A deterministic MVP that reduces stockouts and increases basket value using data you already have.

1. “Don’t Run Out” Core Alerting

  • Threshold-based, no new ML infra.
  • Fast movers per store via revenue × frequency (≥3 months) and recency.
  • Simple heuristic: if last order ≈ 30 ± 7 days → surface in “Don’t run out”.
  • Use scan + order history to estimate rough days of cover.

2. Smart Reorder List (Pre-built Cart)

  • Pre-populate with all fast movers and medium-velocity brands last ordered > 2 months ago.
  • Default quantities = last successful order size.
  • Carlos reviews and taps “Confirm” instead of building from scratch.

3. Inline “High School Picks” v0

  • For “near high school” stores, show top 5 sweet/snack SKUs in similar stores.
  • Rules-based; aggregate network insights, no personalization yet.

4. Safe Experiment Guardrail

  • Max 3 new SKUs per order, 1 case each.
  • After 60 days: mark as “worked / didn’t work” based on sell-through.

5. Why This MVP Ships in 0–6 Weeks

  • Uses existing data and rep workflows; no org changes.
  • Minimal API surface: /recommended_reorder?store_id=1289, /top_trending?segment=high_school_snacks.
  • No new logistics or routing complexity.

Key Metrics

  • Reorder frequency per store.
  • Stockout rate on fast movers.
  • Median time-to-order.
  • Basket size & incremental GMV from medium-velocity SKUs.
  • Experiment success rate & dead-stock rate.
Beyond MVP · Smarter Recommendations
Slide 4 · V2 – Smarter Recs & Autopilot

More Signal, Less Guesswork for Carlos

V2 turns Smart Reorder into a richer decision engine and introduces a path to subscriptions.

1. Better Stock-out Modeling

  • Use rolling 90-day demand + scan frequency to estimate days of cover.
  • Flag risk earlier and rank items by stockout impact × margin.

2. Store-level Personalization

  • Teen-heavy stores (like 1289): favor sweet snacks & beverages in discovery modules.
  • Office-heavy stores: favor coffee, protein, functional HBW.

3. Two-store Orchestration

  • Let Carlos view per-store recommendations side by side.
  • Quick action: “Mirror 1289 order to 22401 (×2)” when it makes sense.

4. Discovery Outcomes Surfaced

  • Simple “Experiment performance” card, e.g.:
    “You tried 8 new items last quarter → 3 made money, 2 broke even, 3 tied up capital. We recommend keeping A, B; dropping C, D, E.”

5. Autopilot for Fast Movers (Subscription / V2)

  • “Keep 21 days of stock for Realsy — auto reorder when needed.”
  • System triggers reorder; Carlos just confirms payment.
  • Addresses P1 (timing) and P3 (time pressure) with very high impact.

Your AI Reorder Assistant
Slide 5 · AI Sidekick (Future)

“What Should I Restock Today?”

Voice-first assistant that turns manual browsing into AI-guided, bundle-based reordering.

Experience States

  • State 1 – Listening: “Hey Sidekick, what should I reorder today?”
  • State 2 – Analysis: checks stockout risk, velocity, trends, peer spikes, seasonality.
  • State 3 – Summary: cards for “Don’t run out”, “Maximize profit”, “Trending near you”, “Weather demand alert”.

What the AI Sidekick Does

  • Predicts stockouts and shows “days left” per SKU.
  • Highlights high-margin SKUs and brand-funded promotions.
  • Surfaces local trends and seasonal / weather spikes.
  • Builds personalized bundles Carlos can confirm with one tap or voice.

Key Modules

  • Low-stock predictor – velocity × scan × seasonality with confidence threshold.
  • High-margin recommendations – margin × units sold and uplift vs current assortment.
  • Trend & micro-segment intelligence – neighborhood type, Google Maps meta, peer demand.
  • Weather & seasonal adjustments – heatwaves, rain, sports seasons.
  • Voice interaction – “reorder my best-sellers”, “show high-margin picks”, “what’s trending near me?”.

Edge Cases

  • No history → fall back to store type + demographic defaults.
  • Multi-store owners → prompt for location.
  • Low-confidence suggestions → ask rep to verify.
  • Owner disagrees → feedback loop into ranking models.
Slide 6 · Interview-style Summary

How This Reorder System Serves Carlos — and RepRally

I designed the reorder experience around Carlos’ real behavior: fast movers he must protect, teen trends he wants but can’t research, and experiments he fears because of dead stock.

The Flow in One View

  • Smart Core Reorder eliminates thinking around timing and quantities.
  • High School Trends drives relevant, high-margin discovery for his teen-heavy store.
  • Safe Experimentation Guardrails bound risk while still growing basket value.
  • Two-store Mode keeps both locations in sync without overcomplicating decisions.
  • Autopilot & AI Sidekick are the natural evolution for retention and operating leverage.

How This Moves the Flywheel

  • More high-quality reorders → more behavioral and demand data.
  • Better data → smarter recommendations and pricing.
  • Smarter recommendations → higher GMV, margin, and store loyalty.
  • Higher GMV & retention → stronger brand adoption and more SKUs to optimize.
